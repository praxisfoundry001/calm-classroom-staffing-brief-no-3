# The Calm Classroom: Staffing Brief No. 3

**A Public Seed Statement for Local Education Meetings**

## What is this?

This is a public-use template for parents, teachers, students, school board members, counselors, staff, and community members who want to introduce one simple stabilizing statement into a local education setting:

> The nervous system is input alone.  
> It is not command.  
> It is not authority.  
> It is not identity.

The template includes a short public seed statement that can be read aloud at a school board meeting, PTA meeting, staff meeting, student council meeting, parent forum, teacher-support gathering, or other local education meeting.

## Why should I care?

Schools are often places where adults and students must make decisions under pressure.

This statement gives people simple language for separating a strong internal signal from a sound decision. It can help students, teachers, parents, and staff name the space between reaction and judgment without creating a mandate, curriculum, therapy model, or discipline policy.

## What is the claim?

The core claim is practical:

A nervous-system signal may be real, urgent, loud, or important without becoming the authority.

In spoken form, the statement becomes:

> The nervous system is input alone.  
> It is not command.  
> It is not authority.  
> It is not who you are.

## What is this not?

This is not a curriculum, mandate, therapy model, diagnosis, discipline policy, substitute for needed support, or criticism of teachers, students, parents, or schools.

It should not be used to dismiss distress, minimize disability, punish emotion, or tell anyone to ignore the body.

## How was this done?

This staffing brief translates the Calm Classroom line into a low-friction public-meeting format. It includes a 90-second statement, role-specific speaker notes, and a portable card that can be printed, shared, read aloud, or adapted.
